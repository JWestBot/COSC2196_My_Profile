Interest in IT

When I was a child my family had a Vic-20 which was upgraded to a Commodore-64, but it was a family friend who was a computer technician that first introduced me to computers properly, explaining hardware and DOS commands, and I've been exploring the world of IT since and it is everywhere.

I currently work in an IT related position as a Business Systems Analyst at RMIT University and have been working with computers for over 20 years, but I don't have any formal qualifications. So it seems like the best opportunity I have ever had to chase away some self-doubt, fill in some knowledge gaps, learn some new things, ensure my career doesn’t stagnate and keep my skills relevant.

By studying at RMIT, I feel like I’m supporting the University that is currently supporting me as an employee, it was an easy choice to study at RMIT.

I’m a bit of an all-rounder, I know how to do a lot of things in the world of IT, but I’m not a master at any of them. I’m hoping through the Bachelor of Information Technology I discover an area that piques my interest and I can work at mastering it.
